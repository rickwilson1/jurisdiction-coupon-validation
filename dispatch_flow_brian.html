Agromin · OCWR Program · Order Dispatch — Version 1

Phase 1: Automation of Order Intake and Notification Flow

Phase 1: Takes every OCWR order that lands in the sales inbox and automatically sends the right instructions to the right people — without anyone at Agromin having to read the email and decide what to do.
Phase 2: Gives Greg a tool to log confirmed delivery dates, automatically sends the customer a scheduling confirmation, and gives Ofelia a live view of the delivery schedule — replacing the manual spreadsheet tracking and Friday email.
⚠ Critical Requirement — SMTP Authentication
The dispatch service runs on Google Cloud and must authenticate with Microsoft 365 to send email from dispatch@agromin.com. This requires Authenticated SMTP to be enabled on the mailbox — a one-time task that cannot be done through the M365 admin UI and requires a Global Admin to run a single PowerShell command:
Set-CASMailbox -Identity dispatch@agromin.com -SmtpClientAuthenticationDisabled $false
Once enabled, an app password must be generated for the mailbox and stored as an environment variable in Cloud Run. Phase 1 cannot send any email until this is done. This must be completed by your M365 Global Admin or MSP before go-live.

External · CIMcloud + Coupon Validator
Customer places order — coupon already validated at checkout
The customer enters their address and coupon code at shop.agromin.com. The coupon validator confirms the coupon is valid for that address and jurisdiction before the order is accepted. By the time the order is placed, validation is complete.
External · CIMcloud
CIMcloud sends order confirmation to sales@agromin.com
Subject: "Pending Approval for Order Number XXXXXX". Program orders include a "Coupon Code:" field in the email body. Paid orders do not — this is the only filter needed.
Automated · Power Automate
Inbox watch detects the order & passes it to dispatch
Monitors sales@agromin.com. Triggers only when: sender is CIMcloud, subject starts with "Pending Approval for Order Number", and body contains "Coupon Code:". Parses all order fields and POSTs them to Cloud Run.
Automated · Cloud Run → Firestore
Order logged & routing determined
No re-validation needed — the coupon was already confirmed at checkout. Cloud Run reads the shipping method to determine routing (delivery vs. pickup) and writes the order record to Firestore — capturing customer name, address, material, quantity, coupon code, fulfillment type, and timestamp.

Pickup path
Pickup order
Shipping method does not contain "Delivery".

Under 5 cu yds: customer self-loads using 5-gallon buckets. Customer scans a QR code at the yard to self-submit their fulfillment.

5 cu yds or more: OCWR staff loads with equipment — truck or trailer required. OCWR logs fulfillment on the Greenery Log.
Delivery path
Delivery order
Shipping method contains "Delivery".

Customer is notified that Agromin will contact them within 1 business day. Greg Jackson receives an alert with full order details to begin scheduling. Order is also added to the Delivery Request Workbook in SharePoint.
Automated · Cloud Run → M365 SMTP
Emails sent automatically from dispatch@agromin.com
Customer receives the correct instructions for their order type (pickup self-load, pickup staff-load, or delivery acknowledgment). Ofelia is CC'd on every customer email.

Delivery orders only: Greg Jackson receives a separate alert email from dispatch@agromin.com with the customer name, phone, delivery address, material, and quantity.

Order status in Firestore is updated to emails_sent once outbound messages are confirmed.

Replies from customers go to dispatch@agromin.com — not back into the shared sales inbox.
Manual · Greg Jackson
Greg Jackson contacts customer & schedules delivery
Greg calls the customer to agree on a date and time window, accounting for customer availability and truck/hauler scheduling. This step is manual — customer availability cannot be automated. Greg records the confirmed slot in the Delivery Request Workbook (SharePoint). Every Friday, Greg emails Ofelia with next week's confirmed delivery schedule.
Automated · Cloud Run → Firestore · On Demand
PDF manifest generated for hauler
A delivery manifest PDF is available on request for any delivery order. Data is pulled directly from Firestore — includes customer name, delivery address, material, quantity, coupon code, jurisdiction, and signature lines for hauler and OCWR staff.
Manual · Hauler + OCWR
Delivery fulfilled
Hauler picks up material from yard. OCWR staff signs the manifest and logs the delivery on the Greenery Log at the time of material pickup. Hauler delivers to customer. Agromin monitors fulfillment monthly via OCWR Greenery Log receipts.
