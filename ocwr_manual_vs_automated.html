Step-by-step — what happens after an order is placed
Current — manual process
Automated — Cloud Run + Power Automate
Step 01
Order confirmation email arrives at sales@agromin.com
Sits in shared inbox. Kendall or Emily sees it next time they check mail — could be hours, or next business day for after-hours orders.
Kendall / Emily
instant
Step 01
Power Automate trigger fires on new email
Trigger fires within seconds of CIMcloud sending the confirmation. No human needed to initiate processing.
Automation
Step 02
Manually read and parse the order
Kendall/Emily reads the email, identifies the coupon code, checks the shipping method, looks up the quantity, and decides which workflow applies.
Kendall / Emily
eliminated
Step 02
Order fields extracted and routed automatically
Power Automate parses coupon code, shipping method, and quantity from the email. Cloud Run applies routing logic: pickup vs. delivery, qty threshold, jurisdiction.
Automation
Step 03
Manually select and send the correct email template
Must choose between "under 5 yards" or "over 5 yards" template, fill in order details, add the right yard address and hours, and send. One wrong choice = customer shows up to the wrong process.
Kendall / Emily
eliminated
Step 03
Correct template selected and dispatched automatically
Cloud Run selects the template based on qty threshold, populates all fields (order #, yard address, hours, coupon), and sends via Microsoft Graph from sales@agromin.com. Deterministic — no human judgment required.
Automation
Step 04
Manually CC Ofelia on every outbound email
Must remember to CC ofelia@agromin.com on every order. If forgotten, Ofelia loses visibility into active orders and cannot coordinate with yards.
Kendall / Emily
eliminated
Step 04
Ofelia CC is hardcoded into every dispatch
CC to ofelia@agromin.com is baked into the email handler. Cannot be forgotten. All order types — pickup and delivery — include her automatically.
Automation
Step 05
Delivery orders manually forwarded to Greg
Kendall/Emily must identify delivery orders, forward to greg@agromin.com, and manually add the delivery address to the SharePoint workbook. Prone to delays and omissions.
Kendall / Emily + Greg
eliminated
Step 05
Greg auto-alerted; SharePoint row written automatically
Cloud Run detects delivery flag, sends Greg a structured alert with all order details, and writes the row to OCWR-Agromin Deliveries.xlsx via Graph API — all before anyone opens their inbox.
Automation → Greg
Step 06
Greg manually compiles weekly delivery schedule
Every Friday, Greg reviews all delivery orders from the week, assembles a schedule, and emails Ofelia. Time-consuming, error-prone, and dependent on Greg being available.
Greg
eliminated
Step 06
Friday schedule auto-compiled and sent to Ofelia
Cloud Scheduler triggers the delivery schedule endpoint every Friday at 4pm. Queries Firestore for the week's delivery orders, formats a summary, and sends it to Ofelia automatically. Greg reviews rather than compiles.
Automation → Greg reviews
Step 07
Manifest slips filled out manually in Word
Greg or OCWR staff fills in the Manifest Slip.docx template for each delivery — order number, customer, material, hauler signature line. No consistent format, stored inconsistently.
Greg / OCWR staff
eliminated
Step 07
PDF manifest auto-generated per delivery order
Cloud Run generates a structured PDF manifest the moment a delivery order is ingested. Consistent format every time. Greg or hauler prints or downloads — no manual data entry.
Automation → Greg prints
Step 08
No order audit trail
Order history lives in email threads and a shared spreadsheet. No way to query how many orders were processed, which jurisdictions are active, or whether a specific order was handled correctly.
—
new
Step 08
Full order log in Firestore
Every order is written to Firestore with order number, coupon, jurisdiction, routing decision, and timestamp. Queryable for monthly OCWR reporting and abuse detection.
Automation

What stays manual — by design
Physical ID verification
Resident must present proof of address at the yard. Cannot be automated — it's the program's fraud control layer.
Material loading
Self-load or OCWR staff — physical labor at the yard. Greenery log signed off by yard staff.
Coupon exception support
Kendall/Emily handle edge cases: codes not working, address mismatches, customer questions. Volume drops sharply once routing is automated.
