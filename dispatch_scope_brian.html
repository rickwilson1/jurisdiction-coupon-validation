How Delivery Orders Are Handled

For review by Brian and the operations team — April 2026
Overview
What this system does

When a customer places a free compost or mulch order on shop.agromin.com using a program coupon code, the order dispatch system automatically identifies it, routes it, and sends the customer the right instructions — with no manual intervention required for the intake step.

For delivery orders specifically: the system handles intake and notification. Brian continues to own scheduling — contacting the customer, agreeing on a date/time, and coordinating the haul. That step is intentionally left manual in version 1.

What happens when a delivery order comes in
1
Customer places order on shop.agromin.com
Uses a jurisdiction coupon code (e.g. COUOCCOM26). Selects a delivery shipping method. CIMcloud sends a confirmation email to sales@agromin.com.
Automated — no change from today
2
Power Automate detects the order email
Watches the sales inbox. Detects emails with subject "Pending Approval for Order Number…" that contain a coupon code. Parses the order details and sends them to the dispatch service.
Automated
3
Dispatch service routes the order
Confirms the coupon is a valid active program code. Detects "Delivery" in the shipping method. Logs the order to the Firestore database as a pending delivery.
Automated
4
Customer receives an acknowledgment email
Sent automatically from sales@agromin.com. Sets the expectation that an Agromin representative will contact them within 1 business day to schedule. Does not promise a date or time.
Automated
5
Brian receives a delivery alert email
Sent simultaneously to greg@agromin.com. Contains the customer name, delivery address, phone number, material, and quantity. This is Brian's signal to contact the customer and coordinate a delivery slot.
Automated
6
Brian contacts the customer and schedules the delivery
Brian calls or emails the customer to agree on a date and time window, accounting for truck availability and customer schedule. This negotiation is not automated — it requires human judgment.
Manual — Brian owns this step
7
Hauler carries a PDF manifest on delivery day
The system can generate a PDF delivery manifest on demand — includes customer name, delivery address, material, quantity, coupon code, and a signature line for OCWR staff. Brian or Ofelia can request this for any delivery order.
Automated — on demand

Note on future versions

Version 1 is intentionally scoped to handle intake and notification only. Once the team has used it for a season, it will be clear whether adding a scheduling step — for example, a simple web form where Brian records a confirmed delivery date, or a view for Ofelia showing pending vs. scheduled deliveries — is worth building. That decision doesn't need to be made now.

Summary — what changes for Brian
✓ What changes
  • Brian receives a structured alert email for every new delivery order — no more scanning the sales inbox manually
  • Customer has already been notified before Brian calls — no need to send a separate acknowledgment
  • Order details (address, material, qty, phone) arrive pre-parsed and ready to act on
  • PDF manifest available on request for the hauler
→ What stays the same
  • Brian still contacts the customer directly to agree on a date and time
  • Brian still manages truck assignment and haul logistics
  • No new software for Brian to log into or learn
  • Scheduling happens the same way it does today
